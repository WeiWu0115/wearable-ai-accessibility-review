What This Review Maps

Health and Neurodiversity

Identify cognitive, sensory, motor, perceptual, emotional, and environmental access needs, plus support strategies from clinical, rehabilitation, occupational therapy, education, digital health, and assistive technology literature.

HCI, XR, and Wearable AI

Translate those needs into interaction techniques, design implications, evaluation criteria, and accessibility principles for smart glasses, wearable sensors, AR/XR, gaze-aware systems, and adaptive AI assistants.

CAWI Framework

CAWI reframes accessibility as a property of the full embodied AI loop, where cognition, perception, bodily action, real-world context, and AI inference all become part of interaction.

Contextual PerceivabilityInformation remains perceivable across changing light, noise, movement, crowding, and physical context.
Sensory ModulationUsers can regulate channel, intensity, timing, color, sound, vibration, motion, and frequency.
Cognitive LegibilityPrompts, task state, AI assistance, and recovery paths are understandable and manageable.
Body-Capability-Aware OperabilityInteraction respects fatigue, posture, tremor, reach, balance, and alternative input needs.
Attention RespectSystems avoid unsafe, intrusive, or cognitively harmful interruptions.
Adaptive TransparencyAdaptations are visible, adjustable, reversible, and under user control.
Inference AccountabilityAI inferences about cognition, attention, fatigue, or intent are correctable and non-pathologizing.

Research Questions

  • What cognitive, sensory, motor, perceptual, emotional, and environmental access needs are relevant to wearable intelligence?
  • What intervention, accommodation, adaptation, or support strategies have been proposed for these needs?
  • How have HCI, XR, wearable computing, and accessible computing translated these needs into design principles or system features?
  • What new accessibility principles are needed when AI systems sense, infer, and adapt to users' cognition, perception, bodily action, and real-world context?
